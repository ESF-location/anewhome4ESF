About the ESF

The European School of Frankfurt (ESF) is an EU‑accredited school in Frankfurt am Main that has outgrown its current site (over 1700 students compared to a capacity of approx. 800). For many years, city and regional politics have been discussing where a new or expanded campus should be built. The German Federal Government, the State of Hessen and the City of Frankfurt are obliged to provide the ground for the ESF, as well as build the school, according to a Treaty signed in 2022. A subsequent German law defined the split of responsibilities and costs between the Federal Government, the State of Hessen, the City of Frankfurt and the ECB.

The city of Frankfurt and the Federal Government seemed to have already agreed, in 2022 and 2023, on moving the ESF to an available ground in the Ratsweg area. The plot is currently used by the so-called Dippmess. The committment to offer to ground in the Ratsweg area was formalised in the German application to host AMLA. To win the AMLA seat, Germany argued that "to show its appreciation and support for the growing European School, Frankfurt has offered a new and very attractive site for the construction of a bigger facility with its “Ratsweg” property". Since then, the ruling coalition in Frankfurt (SPD, Greens, Volt) have backtraced and now remain cautions or even oppose the Ratwsweg solution. strong>

This page focuses on one single question: which location do local parties support for the future ESF campus? It summarises public information on the political positions regarding different location options, as well as answers provided by political parties to my direct enquiries.

Location options under discussion

Several potential locations have been discussed in local politics and the media. The list below is not exhaustive, but covers the main options mentioned in recent debates.

Festplatz am Ratsweg (Ratswegkreisel)

One of the main options considered by the city for a new ESF building, located at the current fairground site used by the Dippemess funfair. Technical and legal studies indicate that a school building would be feasible here, but it would affect existing uses such as the funfair and sports facilities.

This is the preferred solution of FDPstrong>, as the technical studies are already completed and construction can begin promptly. All other parties are currently sceptical due to the implications for the Dippmess.

Länderweg

The CDU party has recently proposed that an area, currently allocated to private gardens and part of the Frankfurt Green Belt, is reallocated to the ESF. The area is close to the S-bahn stop Mühlberg and at a short commuting distance from the ECB Main Building.

This is the preferred solution of CDU, and may have the support of FDP and Freie Wähler based on their response to my inquiry strong>.The proposal is likely to be controversial because the Greens have opposed any decision affecting private gardens, and Ms Weber has been quoted arguing that she will oppose the selection of grounds that are privately owned and require financial outflows for the city of Frankfurt.

New “Stadtteil der Quartiere” (North‑West)

A planned new residential district in the north‑west of Frankfurt, not far from the current ESF site in Niederursel. Integrating the ESF into this new district could preserve school routes, but construction would start late in the decade, delaying the urgently needed expansion.

Press reports that this may be the preferred solution of SPD and Greensstrong>. The proposal implies that there will not be a solution for the ESF until 2030 the earliest.

Offenbach / Kaiserlei area

An alternative location on the Frankfurt–Offenbach border around the Kaiserlei junction. This option would keep the school in the metropolitan area and might offer available land, but moving the ESF partly outside Frankfurt is politically sensitive.

BFF supports this proposal , as the ground is available and still very close to the ECB Main Building. Other parties have opposed as they prefer keeping the school on Frankfurt grounds.

Binding brewery

The Binding brewery closed in 2023, leaving ~6 hectares vacant near the Henninger Turm area; Radeberger had it assessed as viable for ESF needs, citing its central location and transport links. However, the site would have to be purchased from private owners at market prices.

Freie Wähler support this option, and, based on the answers to my inquiry, CDU and FDP may also agree strong>. Ms Weber has been quoted arguing that she will oppose the selection of grounds that are privately owned and require financial outflows for the city of Frankfurt.

Other ideas and temporary solutions (e.g. container classrooms, extensions on the current campus) have also been discussed, but this page focuses on the main long‑term location options.

Methodology & Sources

Positions are summarised from answer to direct enquiries as well as publicly available documents such as:

  • Party press releases and official statements about the ESF location.
  • City of Frankfurt and Offenbach documents and council debates on the ESF site.
  • Local media coverage (e.g. Frankfurter Rundschau, FAZ, Hessenschau) on the ESF location debate.

Three parties have followed-up to my inquiries with public actions:

  • FDP organised an event on 19 February to discuss the future of ESF. The position expressed in the event are reflected in the table above.
  • CDU Frankfurt leader, Dr. Kößler, released a new proposal in an interview with FNP .
  • BFF raised a question in the last city council, criticising the delays and asking if Offenbach was a solution. FNP covered the matter in an article on 30 January. The article is not available online.
In addition, SDP education head, Ms Weber, released an interview with Frankfurt Rundschau explaining the SPD approach. If you are a party representative and notice an error, please contact me for a correction.
